# Project Context

## Title
Human–AI Collaboration in Hiring Decisions: Understanding Trust, Transparency, and Inclusion

## Purpose
This qualitative study explores how hiring professionals perceive and interact with algorithmic decision-support tools.  
We aim to understand:
1. How humans calibrate trust in AI recommendations.  
2. How transparency and explanations shape perceived fairness.  
3. How these systems affect accessibility and inclusion for people with disabilities.

## Participants
Twelve hiring managers and HR professionals from mid-sized technology firms in the United States.

## Data Collection
Semi-structured interviews conducted via Zoom (30–45 minutes each).  
Topics included experience with hiring tools, AI trust, efficiency, fairness, and accessibility.

## Ethics
All participants provided informed consent.  
Identifying information was removed or pseudonymized.  
The study adheres to institutional IRB #2023-041 guidelines.

## Intended Use
Uploaded transcripts and codebook are used solely for workshop demonstration and educational training in transparent, ethical qualitative analysis.

## Outputs
- Thematic code summaries  
- Analytic memos aligned with MERIT and RC frameworks  
- Example reflexivity statements
